Step 1 - Chart of accounts

QBO: Reports → Account List → Export to CSV. Creates your QBO accounts here (bank → bank, credit card → card, income/expense → categories). Sub-accounts ("Expenses:Shop:Supplies") import by their last name ("Supplies").

Step 2 - Transactions

QBO: Reports → Transaction Detail by Account → set your date range, accounting method Cash → Customize → columns must include Date, Transaction Type, Name, Memo/Description, Account, Split, Amount → Export to CSV.
Rows land in Review with your QBO categories already applied (from the Split column). Transfers appear once per account and the second copy gets flagged as a duplicate - Skip it there.

Step 3 - Customers

QBO: Sales → Customers → export icon → Export to Excel, then save/convert as CSV (or copy into a CSV). Needs a customer/name column; email, phone, address come along if present.

Step 4 - Mileage

QBO: Mileage → download/export trips as CSV. Needs date + distance columns.

Step 5 - Opening balances

Enter each account's real-world balance as of the day before your transaction export starts (from the bank/card statement). Banks: money in the account. Cards: amount owed. Posted against Owner's Equity. Do this once, after step 2 is reviewed and posted.

Step 6 - Verify

After posting everything in Review: each account's balance on the Dashboard should match the real bank/card balance, and the P&L for last year should match the same report in QBO (cash basis). When they match, your books have officially moved out - cancel the QBO subscription.
